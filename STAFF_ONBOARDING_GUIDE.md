## 📊 DASHBOARD OVERVIEW

The dashboard has **8 main sections** (tabs):

### 1. 📈 **Dashboard** (Default)
Your command center. Shows:
- Pipeline summary (tenders by status)
- Recently active tenders
- Alerts & action items
- Quick statistics

**Action:** Review "EP Action Board" section — these are items needing your attention first

---

### 2. 🎯 **Discovery** (Find Tenders)
Shows newly discovered tenders that match your search criteria.

**What you'll see:**
- 30–50 new tenders daily
- Scoring information (why were they selected?)
- Strategic alignment notes

**What to do:**
- Click "View Details" to read full tender info
- Click "Add to Pipeline" to track it
- Click "Not relevant" if it's a bad match (helps us learn)

**Pro tip:** Most tenders are already scored automatically. High-scoring ones (80+) are your best opportunities.

---

### 3. 📋 **Pipeline** (Bid Status)
Tracks all tenders you're working on, organized by status:

**Status flow:**
```
Interested → Qualifying → Bid Draft → Bid Review → Submitted → Won/Lost
```

**What to do:**
- Click any tender to view full details
- Update status as you progress
- View bid sections already written
- See upcoming deadlines (highlighted if <7 days)

**Color coding:**
- 🟢 Green = Won
- 🔵 Blue = In Progress / Open
- ⚫ Gray = Closed / Lost
- 🟠 Orange = Upcoming / Planned

---

### 4. ✍️ **Bid Writer** (AI-Assisted)
The most powerful tool. Generates bid sections with AI.

**How to use:**

1. **Select a tender** from your pipeline
2. **Choose a section type** (16 available):
   - Company Overview
   - Experience & Track Record
   - Management Approach
   - Staffing & Qualifications
   - Social Value
   - Innovation
   - Quality Assurance
   - Pricing Strategy
   - Risk Management
   - And 7 more...

3. **Click "Generate Section"**
   - AI reads the tender and writes a section
   - Takes 2–3 seconds
   - Always cites specific requirements from the tender

4. **Review & Improve**
   - Read what AI wrote
   - Use "Improve" button for quick rewrites
   - Chat with EP Agent to refine specific points
   - Copy/paste to Word/Google Docs when ready

**Advanced Features:**
- 📚 Learning Vault — Save lessons from past bids
- 🔍 Evidence Picker — Attach supporting documents
- 💬 Elizabeth Chat — Ask for specific improvements
- ⭐ Bid Scoring — Get a 1–10 score before submitting

**Quality tip:** Always review what AI writes. It's 90%+ accurate but needs your expertise for final polish.

## 🚀 YOUR FIRST WORKFLOW (30 Minutes)

### Step 1: Find a Tender (5 min)
1. Click "Discovery" tab
2. Find a tender that interests you (or click "Finder" to search)
3. Review the summary and deadline
4. Click "View Full Details"

### Step 2: Add to Pipeline (2 min)
1. Click "Add to Pipeline"
2. Select status: "Interested" (default) or "Qualifying"
3. Save — it now appears on your Pipeline tab

### Step 3: Review with Elizabeth (10 min)
1. Click 🤖 Elizabeth chat
2. Ask: "Summarize the key requirements"
3. Ask: "What's their top 3 evaluation criteria?"
4. Take notes on scoring approach

### Step 4: Generate First Bid Section (10 min)
1. Click "Bid Writer" tab
2. Select the same tender
3. Choose "Company Overview" section
4. Click "Generate Section"
5. Read what AI wrote
6. If good: Copy to Word or click "Improve"
7. If needs work: Click "Improve" and choose an option

### Step 5: Review Score (3 min)
1. Click "Score Bid" button
2. Review the 1–10 score
3. Click feedback items to improve
4. Go back and improve any weak sections

**Congratulations!** You've completed your first bid section. 🎉

## 🔧 COMMON TASKS

### Task: Generate All 16 Sections
**Time:** ~45 minutes for average tender

1. Click "Bid Writer"
2. Select tender
3. Click "Generate All" (bulk action)
4. AI generates all sections in ~2 minutes
5. Review each section (2-3 min each)
6. Improve any that need work
7. Submit to governance

**Pro tip:** Generate all first, then improve, then submit. Faster workflow.

---

### Task: Upload Evidence (Supporting Docs)
**To attach case studies, testimonials, certifications:**

1. In Bid Writer, click "Evidence Picker" (on a section)
2. Click "Upload Document"
3. Choose file (PDF, Word, image)
4. AI reads it and suggests where to cite it
5. Click "Insert Citation" in your section text

**Types to upload:**
- Case studies (one per file)
- Testimonials/references
- Certifications & awards
- Org charts
- Financial statements

---

### Task: Learn from Past Bids
**To capture lessons:**

1. After you win or lose a tender, go to "Knowledge" tab
2. Click "Add Lesson"
3. Enter: What was the bid about, What worked, What to do next time
4. AI stores this in your "Learning Vault"
5. Next time you write a similar bid, Elizabeth references your lessons

**Example:** If you always score high on "Social Value", Elizabeth will suggest your proven approach for new tenders.

---

### Task: Request Help from Your Manager
**To send a bid for review:**

1. In Bid Writer, click "Submit to Governance"
2. Choose reviewer (your manager)
3. Add a note ("Please review company overview")
4. Click "Send"
5. Manager gets an email with your sections
6. Manager can give feedback or approve

**Time:** Usually 4–24 hours for feedback

## 🆘 TROUBLESHOOTING

### Problem: I can't log in
**Solution:**
1. Check you're using the right URL
2. Verify email is exactly as sent (case sensitive for some systems)
3. Use "Forgot Password" to reset
4. If still stuck, contact IT support email

### Problem: Dashboard is blank / showing errors
**Solution:**
1. Refresh page (Ctrl+R or Cmd+R)
2. Clear browser cache (Settings → Clear browsing data)
3. Try a different browser
4. Contact support if still blank

### Problem: AI bid sections are not generating
**Solution:**
1. Check your internet connection
2. Wait 10 seconds and try again (our system may be reloading)
3. If error mentions "budget exceeded": Your org has hit AI spend cap — contact admin
4. If error mentions "provider unavailable": System is briefly down — wait 5 min and retry

### Problem: Emails are too many / too few
**Solution:**
1. Go to Settings → Notifications
2. Toggle specific email types on/off
3. Examples:
   - Turn off "Daily Digest" if you prefer manual checking
   - Turn on "Weekly Summary" for high-level view only

### Problem: Can't find a specific tender I'm looking for
**Solution:**
1. Use "Finder" tab → Search by:
   - Buyer name (e.g., "FCDO")
   - Keyword (e.g., "event")
   - Value range
   - Source
2. Adjust date range if it's an old tender
3. Try partial matches (e.g., "FCDo" instead of full "Foreign Commonwealth Development Office")

### Problem: Score is too low (need to improve bid)
**Solution:**
1. Click "Score Bid" again to see specific feedback
2. Ask Elizabeth: "How can I improve [section name]?"
3. Use "Improve" button on that section
4. Re-score after changes
5. Typical path: 6 → 7 → 8 with 2-3 rounds of improvement

## ⭐ BEST PRACTICES

### Writing Better Bids
1. **Use specific examples** — AI generates generic; add your real case studies
2. **Match buyer language** — If tender says "resilient", use "resilient" in your bid
3. **Support numbers with evidence** — "200+ events delivered" should link to customer cases
4. **Address evaluation criteria directly** — If they score on "Value", your entire bid should emphasize value
5. **Keep it concise** — Most buyers scan; tighten your language

### Using Elizabeth Effectively
1. **Ask specific questions** — Better: "How do I strengthen the compliance section?" vs "Make this better"
2. **Provide context** — Tell her which section or bid you're working on
3. **Follow up** — Ask "Why?" if her suggestion doesn't make sense
4. **Learn from feedback** — Save good Elizabeth responses for future use

### Managing Your Time
1. **Don't spend >3 hours per tender** — If you do, delegate to team or ask manager
2. **Generate first, improve second** — AI is faster than starting blank
3. **Batch work** — Generate all 16 sections, then review them all
4. **Use templates** — If you're repeating the same structure, save it

### Team Collaboration
1. **Share lessons** — When you win, document what worked in Learning Vault
2. **Review peers' bids** — Learning from each other improves overall quality
3. **Ask managers for guidance** — Complex bids? Ask for strategic direction before writing
