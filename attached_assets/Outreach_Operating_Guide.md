# Outreach Operating Guide
## Event & Community Growth Intelligence Platform
### For non-technical team members — how to find, research, approve, and send emails

---

## Quick Overview: Two Lanes

The platform has **two completely separate email lanes**. They never mix up — a partner can never accidentally receive a guest invite, and a guest can never receive a partner pitch.

| Lane | Who | Template | Number of emails | Where to draft |
|------|-----|----------|------------------|----------------|
| **Partner / Sponsor** | Brands, hotels, media, drinks, gifting partners | Locked partner pitch template (no AI invention) | 2 touches: first + one follow-up | **Outreach Control** |
| **Guest** | Individual women invited to attend | Guest invitation template (assembled from approved research fields) | 4-touch sequence | **Outreach** |

## Part 1: The Partner Lane — How to send a partner email

### Example: Muddy Stilettos (Media Partner)

#### Step 1: Find the contact
1. Log in at `https://eventperfektplanningapp.com/growth-platform/`
2. Click **Sponsors** in the left sidebar
3. Click **Outreach Control** from the submenu
4. Select **The Woman Who Leads The Room** from the event dropdown at the top
5. Click **Muddy Stilettos** in the prospect list on the left

#### Step 2: Fill in the research fields (right-hand panel)
These fields power the partner template. If any are blank, the email cannot generate.

| Field | What to put | For Muddy |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Category** | Pick from dropdown: `sponsor`, `media`, `hotel`, `guest` | `sponsor` |
| **Status** | Must be `approved_for_outreach` to send | `approved_for_outreach` |
| **Email verified** | Tick the checkbox | ☑️ (already verified) |
| **Contact route verified** | Tick the checkbox | ☑️ (already verified) |
| **Approved by** | Type a name — e.g. `Lynda` | `Lynda` |
| **Why them** | 1–2 sentences: why this brand fits the room | "Muddy Stilettos reaches exactly the kind of affluent, engaged regional women this room is being built for across Bucks, Beds and Herts" |
| **Specific ask** | The exact commercial opportunity you're offering | "A regional media partnership including a feature, event listing, and pre-event / post-event coverage" |
| **What they receive** | Bullet points of what the partner gets back | First access to story, founder interview, named partner recognition, association with the event |

> 💡 **Tip:** All three of these — *Why them*, *Specific ask*, *What they receive* — must be filled or the system refuses to draft the email. This is the safety gate.

#### Step 3: Open the send gate
Look at the **Send gate** panel. It shows four checkmarks:
- Email Verified ☑️
- Contact Route Verified ☑️
- Approved by Lynda ☑️
- Status = Approved for Outreach ☑️

When all four are green, the gate is **open**.

#### Step 4: Preview the draft (optional but recommended)
Click the **Generate Preview** button in the "Email AI — Preview" panel.  
The system shows you:
- **Subject line**
- **Full email body** (with the partner's name, company, and custom ask merged in)
- **Partner-specific ask** and **benefits** (sanity check)
- **Risk flags** (if any)
- A status badge: "Ready for review" (green) or "Needs human approval" (amber)

Read it carefully. If anything looks wrong, fix the research fields above and click Generate Preview again.

#### Step 5: Send
Two ways to send:

**Option A — From Outreach Control (gate already open):**
1. Click the burgundy **Go to send** button
2. This takes you to the **Outreach** page, Pending tab
3. Find the Muddy draft, click **Approve**
4. Click **Send now** — the email fires immediately

**Option B — From Outreach page directly:**
1. Go to **Audience → Outreach**
2. Select the event
3. In the **Generate** card on the left, pick Muddy from the dropdown
4. Click **Generate 4-touch sequence** (partners get 2 of the 4)
5. The draft lands in the **Pending** tab
6. Click **Show message** to expand and read the full text
7. Click **Approve**, then **Send now**

> 🚨 **Daily cap:** Sponsor lane = 5 emails per day. If you hit the cap, the button greys out until tomorrow.

## Part 2: The Guest Lane — How to send a guest invitation

### Example: Inviting a senior woman to attend the dinner

#### Step 1: Add the guest to the system
There are three ways to get a guest into the system:

**A. Discovery (automated search):**
1. Go to **Audience → Discovery**
2. Select the event
3. Click **Search Prospects**
4. The system searches Apollo.io for matching profiles
5. Review the results — tick the checkbox next to anyone you want to keep
6. Click **Import Selected**

**B. Manual add (one-by-one):**
1. Go to **Audience → Outreach Control**
2. Click **Import CSV** at the top
3. Upload a CSV with columns: `name`, `email`, `company`, `title`
4. The system imports them as `guest` category automatically

**C. Inbound (they applied via the website):**
1. Go to **Intelligence → Inbound**
2. Review submissions from the event landing page
3. Click **Approve** to move them into the outreach pipeline

#### Step 2: Research and lock the guest intelligence
This is the most important step. The guest email is assembled from **approved research fields** — the system does not guess or invent.

1. Go to **Audience → Screen**
2. Find your guest in the list
3. Click on their name to open the detail panel
4. Fill in the **Guest Intelligence** fields:

| Field | What to put | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| **Why this room matters to her** | Why should she care? What makes this room relevant to her career, business, or life? | "As a founder scaling her second business, she needs a peer network of women who have raised capital and built teams" |
| **Room contribution** | What does she bring to the room? Why would other guests benefit from her being there? | "She brings fintech expertise, a warm connector style, and is actively hiring senior women — valuable to other founders in the room" |
| **Guest reason** | The personal hook — one sentence on why she is invited specifically | "You were recommended by Caroline M. as someone who builds exceptional teams and would add depth to the room" |

> 🚨 **These three fields are mandatory.** If any are blank, the system refuses to draft the guest email. This is the hard gate — no guest invite sends without complete, approved intelligence.

#### Step 3: Verify the contact
Back in **Outreach Control** (or on the prospect card in Screen):
- Tick **Email verified**
- Tick **Contact route verified**
- Set **Status** to `approved_for_outreach`
- Type **Approved by:** `Lynda`

#### Step 4: Generate the guest invitation
1. Go to **Audience → Outreach**
2. Select the event on the left
3. In the **Generate** card, pick the guest from the dropdown
4. Click **Generate 4-touch sequence**

The system:
- Loads the approved **master guest template**
- Substitutes the guest's name, company, and the three locked intelligence fields
- Checks that no placeholder is left unresolved
- Checks the word count (max 500, soft guide 350)
- Drops the draft into the **Pending** queue

#### Step 5: Review and send
1. The draft appears in the **Pending** tab
2. Click **Show message** to expand and read the full email
3. You can also click **Why this email** to see the AI trust score (quality check)
4. If it looks good: click **Approve**, then **Send now**
5. If it needs changes: click **Reject**, go back and edit the research fields, then regenerate

> 🚨 **Daily cap:** Guest lane = 20 emails per day.

## Part 3: The Approval Flow — What each status means

| Status | What it means | Who moves it |
|--------|---------------|--------------|
| `new` | Just imported — no research yet | System (on import) |
| `research_needed` | Needs guest intelligence or partner fields filled | You (move to this when research is incomplete) |
| `verified` | Research fields look complete, ready for Lynda review | You |
| `approved_for_outreach` | Lynda has approved — can now generate drafts | You (type "Lynda" in Approved by field) |
| `pending` | Draft generated, waiting for human click | System (auto on generate) |
| `approved` | You clicked Approve — ready to send | You |
| `sent` | Email left the building | System (auto on Send now) |
| `replied` | They replied — no more auto-follow-ups | System (auto on reply detection) |
| `do_not_contact` | Suppressed or requested stop | You |

## Part 5: Safety rules (built into the system)

1. **No auto-send.** Every email requires a human click. The system drafts; you approve and send.
2. **Guest template is locked.** It only uses the three approved research fields. The AI cannot invent reasons or guess at personal details.
3. **Partner template is locked.** It uses the partner's specific ask, what they receive, and why them. No generic "we'd love to partner" language.
4. **Unresolved placeholder check.** If any `{{field}}` is not filled, the draft is refused.
5. **Suppression check.** If the email is on a suppression list, the send is blocked.
6. **Daily caps.** Guest: 20/day. Sponsor: 5/day. Media: 5/day. Hotel: 3/day.
7. **Reply detection.** If someone replies, their follow-up sequence stops automatically.
8. **One email per product per day.** The system enforces this at the send gate.

## Part 6: Common tasks — quick lookup

### I need to check if an email already sent to someone
Go to **Audience → Outreach → Sent** tab. Search or scroll.

### I need to see what a draft looks like before approving
Click **Show message** on any Pending draft. The full body expands below.

### I need to edit a draft after generating
Click **Reject**, go back to Outreach Control, edit the research fields, then regenerate.

### I need to send a follow-up to someone who didn't reply
If they are `in_sequence` status, the system auto-generates follow-ups on a schedule (Tue–Thu, 9am–3pm). You still have to approve each one.

### I need to stop someone from receiving any more emails
Go to **Outreach Control**, set their status to `do_not_contact`. This blocks all future sends.

### I need to add a new sender (not Lynda)
Go to **Settings** (gear icon at bottom of sidebar) → **Senders**. Add name and email. The system will include the new sender in the dropdown.

### I need to see how much we're spending on AI and data enrichment
Go to **Intelligence → Intelligence** tab. Scroll to the Spend section. Every API call is logged with vendor, model, tokens, and cost.

## Summary: The 10-step partner send

1. Go to **Sponsors → Outreach Control**
2. Select the event
3. Click the partner's name
4. Set Category to `sponsor`, Status to `approved_for_outreach`
5. Tick Email verified, Contact route verified
6. Type `Lynda` in Approved by
7. Fill Why them, Specific ask, What they receive
8. Click **Generate Preview** — read the draft
9. Click **Go to send** (or go to **Audience → Outreach → Pending**)
10. Click **Approve**, then **Send now**

## Summary: The 10-step guest send

1. Go to **Audience → Discovery** (or import via CSV)
2. Search and import the guest
3. Go to **Audience → Screen**
4. Click the guest's name
5. Fill Guest intelligence: Why this room matters, Room contribution, Guest reason
6. Go to **Outreach Control** — tick Email verified, Contact route verified, set Status to `approved_for_outreach`, type `Lynda` in Approved by
7. Go to **Audience → Outreach**
8. Select the event, pick the guest from the Generate dropdown
9. Click **Generate 4-touch sequence**
10. In **Pending** tab: click **Show message**, read it, click **Approve**, click **Send now**
