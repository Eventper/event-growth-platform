# I AM HER: The Stories Behind the Room — Build Spec

**Property:** Attaches to the existing I Am Her landing page (eventperfekt.net/iamher)
**Event:** The Woman Who Leads The Room — "I Am Her", Friday 28 August 2026, Milton Keynes
**Purpose:** User-generated story collection + public Story Wall. Builds social proof, organic SEO content, and a soft conversion path to the event.
**Status:** Spec ready for build. Compliance sign-off required before launch (see §8).
**Imagery:** Every section carries at least one relevant image. Imagery is not decoration here — it gives the page light, emotion and life, and signals who this room is for. Specs per section below; global art direction in §0.

---

## 0. Imagery & visual direction (applies to every section)

Photography is the emotional engine of this page. Get it right and the copy lands harder.

**Tone:** Editorial, premium, cinematic — closer to a leadership magazine than a stock library. Warm but serious. Real women, not posed corporate clichés (no fake handshakes, no headset call-centre shots, no laughing-alone-with-salad).

**Subjects:** Professional women — founders, executives, entrepreneurs. **Diverse and inclusive, with Black and diaspora women prominent**, reflecting the actual audience and the TWWLTR/I Am Her brand. Range of ages. Real expressions: composure, reflection, quiet strength, the weight behind the title.

**Mood per emotional beat:** the page moves from *pressure → honesty → connection → invitation*. Imagery should track that arc — heavier and more introspective up top, warmer and more collective toward the CTA.

**Consistency:** One coherent grade across all images — match whatever look already governs eventperfekt.net/iamher (colour palette, warmth, contrast). Don't mix bright stock with moody editorial.

**Rights & safety:** Licensed stock (or commissioned shoots) only — no scraped or unlicensed images. **No identifiable real person's photo attached to a story** unless that person submitted it and consented; story imagery should be evocative/abstract, never implying a stock model *is* the storyteller.

**Performance:** All images optimised (WebP, responsive `srcset`, lazy-load below the fold), with descriptive alt text for accessibility and SEO.

## 2. Story submission form

| Field | Type | Rules |
|---|---|---|
| Full Name | Text input | Required |
| Share Anonymously | Toggle | When ON, triggers anonymity logic (§2.1) |
| Job Title | Text input | Optional |
| Company / Organisation | Text input | Optional — **suppressed on display if Anonymous is ON** (§2.1) |

| Story Category | Dropdown | Required. See category list below |

**Story categories:** Burnout · Identity · Leadership · Reinvention · Pressure · Motherhood & Career · Toxic Organisation · Financial Strain · Spouse / Relationship · Death or Loss · The Woman Behind the Title

**Story placeholder:**
> Tell us about a moment, season or experience that shaped you — burnout, pressure, reinvention, identity, leadership or the woman behind the title.

**Image — form supporting visual (required):** A single warm, encouraging image beside or above the form — a woman writing/journalling, or a calm, intimate "permission to speak" moment. Lighter and more inviting than the hero, signalling safety. Keep it from competing with the form fields for attention.

### 2.1 Anonymity logic (corrected)
If **Share Anonymously** is ON:
- Display name renders as **"Anonymous"**.
- **Company / Organisation is suppressed entirely** on all public surfaces (wall card, full story page, share previews, SEO meta).
- Job Title is generalised on display — store the exact title, but render only a broad descriptor (e.g. "Senior Executive", "Founder", "Finance Leader"). The moderator selects/confirms the generalised descriptor at approval.
- SEO slug must not contain name or company (§6).

> Rationale: "Anonymous CFO at [named mid-size firm]" identifies the person instantly. Anonymity must hold across *all* fields, not just the name.

### 2.2 Support / safeguarding notice (always visible above Submit)
> Sharing can bring up difficult feelings. If you're struggling right now, please reach out to someone you trust or a support service. In the UK you can contact the Samaritans free on 116 123, any time.

### 2.3 Consent + privacy
- **Consent checkbox (required):**
  ☐ I give permission for my story to be shared on The Woman Who Leads The Room website and social channels. I understand I can ask for my story to be removed at any time.
- **Privacy notice link (required):** must state lawful basis (consent), what is collected, that story content may include sensitive personal information shared voluntarily, retention period, and the right to withdraw a published story. Stories and email addresses are processed by Event Perfekt Global Ltd as data controller.

## 6. SEO URLs
Pattern: `/stories/i-am-her-{category}-{descriptor}-{unique}`
- Example: `/stories/i-am-her-burnout-founder-amaka` or `/stories/i-am-her-burnout-founder-7f3k`
- **Must append a unique element** (first name where non-anonymous, else short ID) to prevent collisions between two stories in the same category.
- Anonymous stories: never use name or company in the slug — use category + descriptor + short ID only.

## 7. Admin / moderation

### 7.1 Approval queue (required)
Stories **never auto-publish**. All submissions enter a review queue.
**Status values:** Pending · Approved · Rejected

### 7.2 Featured stories
Admin can mark a story **Featured**. Featured stories pin to the top of the Story Wall.

### 7.3 Search & filter (public)
Users filter the wall by category: Burnout · Leadership · Motherhood & Career · Financial Strain · Identity · etc. (all categories from §2).

### 7.4 Share buttons
Share on LinkedIn · Share on Facebook · Copy Link.

### 7.5 Moderator review rubric (required before any approval)
The reviewer checks each story against these before changing status to Approved:
1. **No named or identifiable organisations** in "Toxic Organisation", "Spouse / Relationship" or any story — defamation/libel risk. Generalise or redact; if it can't be made safe, reject with a note.
2. **No identifiable third parties** (named individuals, employers, partners) presented in a damaging light.
3. **Anonymity integrity** — if Anonymous, confirm company is suppressed and job title generalised (§2.1).
4. **Safeguarding escalation** — if a story contains indications of immediate risk (self-harm intent, ongoing abuse, a child at risk), do **not** simply reject. Hold as Pending, do not publish, and escalate per the internal safeguarding step below. A reply with support signposting should be sent to the submitter's email.
5. **Consent present** — story is not published unless the consent checkbox was ticked at submission.

> **Internal safeguarding step:** a designated reviewer flags any concerning submission to the event lead and the Group Risk & Compliance function. Document the decision. This mirrors the duty-of-care standard the event already sets with a wellbeing authority on stage.

## 8. Compliance & legal (sign-off gate before launch)
This feature collects names, email addresses and voluntarily-shared sensitive personal information (mental health, bereavement, relationships). It must be signed off by the Group Risk & Compliance / MLRO function before going live.

Checklist:
- [ ] Privacy notice published and linked from the form
- [ ] Lawful basis (consent) documented; consent wording matches §2.3
- [ ] Retention and withdrawal process defined (how a submitter requests removal, and who actions it)
- [ ] Email addresses stored securely, never surfaced publicly, never exported to marketing without separate opt-in
- [ ] Moderator rubric (§7.5) issued to whoever holds the review function
- [ ] Defamation rule (no identifiable organisations) enforced in rubric and tested

## 9. Social proof counter (homepage)
> "37 women have already shared their story."

**Build rules:**
- The number must be **dynamic and real** — count of genuine submissions (approved, or approved + pending — decide one and stick to it). **Never hardcoded.**
- Update on submission so the count moves as participation grows.
- If counts are low at launch, consider hiding the counter until it passes a credible threshold rather than displaying a small or static number.

**Image — counter band (required):** A collective image of women (rows of faces, a full room, or a montage) behind or beside the counter to make the social proof visual as well as numeric — "look how many of us there are."

## 10. Acceptance criteria (build is "done" when)
1. Submission form validates required fields + consent before enabling Submit.
2. Anonymity toggle correctly suppresses company and generalises job title across card, full page, share preview and slug.
3. Submissions land in the queue as Pending; nothing publishes without an Approved status set by a moderator.
4. Story Wall renders approved stories, featured pinned to top, category filter working.
5. Full story pages live at unique, collision-proof SEO slugs.
6. Share buttons (LinkedIn, Facebook, Copy Link) functional on full story pages.
7. Support notice (§2.2) visible on the form; privacy notice linked.
8. Homepage counter pulls a live count.
9. CTA to the ticket page present on the wall and on every full story page.
10. Compliance checklist (§8) signed off.
11. Every section carries its required image(s) per §0–§9, all licensed, optimised (WebP/responsive/lazy-load), with descriptive alt text, and graded to one consistent look across the page.
