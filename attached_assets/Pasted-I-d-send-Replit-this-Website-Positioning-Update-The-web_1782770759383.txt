**Website Positioning Update**

The website has drifted towards being a "women's leadership" or "wellbeing" event. That is **not** the positioning.

The positioning should be:

> **A private, invitation-only evening for accomplished women.**

The audience happens to be founders, CEOs, directors, executives and senior professionals, but we are **not** branding this as a women's leadership event, women in business event or founders' community.

### Please make the following changes:

### 1. Hero section

Replace:

> **A private luxury dinner and wellbeing evening...**

With:

> **A private, invitation-only evening for accomplished women.**

Supporting copy:

> **Bringing together 100 carefully selected women from across business, healthcare, finance, the arts and public life for an evening of thoughtful conversation, exceptional hospitality and recognition.**

---

### 2. Remove leadership positioning

Avoid phrases like:

* leadership experience
* leadership dinner
* women leaders
* women in leadership
* leadership community
* executive leadership

Only mention leadership naturally when describing guests or conversations.

---

### 3. Make the room the hero

Move this message much higher on the homepage:

> **What makes this evening different is the room.**

Supporting copy:

> Around the table will be founders, chief executives, board directors and accomplished women from across business, healthcare, finance, the arts and public life. Women who may never ordinarily meet, yet who share the experience of carrying significant responsibility.

---

### 4. Keep the "woman behind the title"

This is one of the strongest messages on the site.

Build around it rather than around wellbeing or leadership.

---

### 5. Reduce repeated wellbeing language

Wellbeing should support the experience.

It should **not** become the main reason people attend.

---

### 6. Keep Dr Sarah's title simple

Use:

> **Dr Sarah Jenkins – Harley Street Women's Health Specialist**

Avoid leading with menopause throughout the site.

---

### 7. Position the evening correctly

It is:

* invitation only
* carefully curated
* limited to 100 women
* premium
* cross-industry
* conversation-led
* beautifully hosted

It is **not**:

* a networking event
* a women-in-business event
* a conference
* a founders' group
* a coaching programme

---

### 8. Make the price feel premium

Use throughout:

> **Attendance is by invitation, with a £360 guest contribution.**

Avoid "ticket price" or "buy ticket."

---

### 9. Keep the tone understated

Avoid marketing language like:

* transformational
* empowering
* community
* movement
* sisterhood

Instead, use language such as:

* thoughtful
* curated
* considered
* invitation-only
* accomplished
* private
* exceptional

---

**Overall objective:**

When someone lands on the website, they should immediately understand that this is **a carefully curated private evening for accomplished women**, not another leadership conference, networking event or wellbeing seminar. The emphasis should be on the calibre of the room, the quality of the conversation and the experience of being invited.
