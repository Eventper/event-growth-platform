Findings:

- ❌ **The two reports disagree** (digest `passedGate=0` vs dashboard `1 qualifying`) — they read different datasets.
- ❌ **Dedup not applied** to the digest (`deduped=0`, exact duplicates + every multi-lot framework doubled).
- ❌ **Deadlines panel unfiltered** — lists demolition, payroll, tree-pruning: the raw market, not EP's bids.

- ❌ **International geography not screened** — a **Thailand** tender passed for a London/Africa-only firm.

## Fix 1 — One report, one source of truth (highest priority)

The digest and the dashboard must never contradict each other.
- Both surfaces read from **one canonical query / view** of "qualifying tenders for org X today" (e.g. a single `qualified_tenders` view or service function). The email and the dashboard both call it; neither computes its own counts.
- The header counters (`raw / passedGate / excludedByKeyword / staleDropped / deduped`) and the rendered rows must derive from the **same** result set — not separate variables (the Phase 1 counter-binding bug, now visible across two surfaces).
- Acceptance: a sweep produces identical qualifying counts and identical tender lists in the digest and the dashboard, every time.

## Fix 2 — Dedup + collapse multi-lot frameworks
- Enforce `UNIQUE (org_id, ocid)` (or `(org_id, source, source_notice_id)` where no OCID) at ingest with `ON CONFLICT DO UPDATE`. Verify the index exists in the **live DB**, not just the schema file.
- Deadlines/qualifying queries select **distinct** on that key — no title appears twice.
- Collapse multi-lot frameworks (e.g. Northumbria T25-0047, 8 lots) to **one** line: `<ref> <title> — <buyer> (N lots)`, earliest deadline; lots accessible on drill-in.

## Fix 3 — Scope the deadlines panel to EP's live bids
- `WHERE org_id=:org AND status IN ('committed','drafting','submitting') AND deadline BETWEEN now() AND now()+:window`.
- Never show raw / excluded / stale / unqualified notices. Empty is the correct state when nothing is committed.

## Fix 4 — Lane discipline: advisory ≠ delivery
The Palladium/ISSB tender exposed that the gate scores keyword-relevance but not **lane fit**. EP's development lane is for **events & programme *delivery***, never aid/advisory consultancy.
- Move to **EXCLUDE (or hard warning flag)**: *institutional capacity building, capacity enhancement, standards adoption, ISSB, technical assistance, advisory services, policy reform, institutional strengthening, sector reform* — i.e. standards/policy/advisory development work.
- Keep qualifying only where an EP delivery keyword co-occurs (*event, workshop, conference, summit, logistics, programme delivery, stakeholder engagement, training event*). Advisory term **without** a delivery term → drop/flag.

## Fix 5 — International geography allow-list
A London/Africa-only firm should never see a Thailand tender qualify.
- Add a geography screen with an **allow-list**: United Kingdom + EP's African footprint (Nigeria, Ghana, Uganda, Senegal, Zambia, South Africa, Sierra Leone, Mozambique, Kenya) + explicit diaspora relevance.
- Any tender whose delivery country is outside the allow-list → **flag `out-of-geography` and drop from qualifying** (or route to human review, never auto-qualify).
- This complements the existing UK place-based locality flag — that one screens *which UK city*; this one screens *which country*.

## Fix 6 — Keyword-exclusion audit (false friends)
- Log **which exclude term fired** per dropped notice.
- One-off: dump the 25 exclusions + matched term; check nothing comms/creative was killed by a blunt rule (watch "campaign" caught by "waste/recycling", e.g. the Merseyside "Recycle Right campaign" — relevant lane, though a correct no-bid on locality/timing anyway).
- Make it a recurring weekly 2-minute review.
