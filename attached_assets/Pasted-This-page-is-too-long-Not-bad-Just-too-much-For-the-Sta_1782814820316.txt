This page is **too long**.

Not bad. Just too much.

For the Stay page, the job is simple:

**make travelling to Milton Keynes feel easy, valuable, and worth staying for.**

Right now it does that, but it’s padded. Padding reduces action.

## What to keep

Keep these sections:

1. **Turn the evening into a weekend**
2. **Why stay in Milton Keynes?**
3. **EP Concierge**
4. **Where to stay**
5. **48 Hours in Milton Keynes**
6. **Pick your weekend style**
7. **Key weekend experiences**
8. **For local businesses**
9. **Supporting the local visitor economy**

That structure works.

The civic/economic part is strong because it clearly says the event brings women from across the UK into Milton Keynes and supports overnight stays, restaurants, retail, attractions and wider city engagement. Keep that section. 

## What to cut or tighten

### 1. “Stay over, bring a friend or family”

Change to:

**Stay over, bring someone with you, and let the EP Concierge help you shape your Milton Keynes weekend.**

“Family” is fine later. Don’t put it in the hero. The event is for senior women, not a family trip.

### 2. “You’re already coming for the room. Why not stay for the weekend?”

Keep it. Strong. Simple.

### 3. “Premium hotels close to the venue”

Careful. If venue is not public yet, say:

**Premium hotels close to central Milton Keynes**

### 4. “Special discounts”

Do not say discounts unless confirmed. Use:

**Guest stay options and local recommendations available for confirmed guests.**

### 5. EP Concierge wording

“Let us help you plan it” is okay, but “concierge” only works if you actually reply fast.

Use:

**Need help planning the weekend?**

Subline:

**The EP Concierge can help with hotel options, group stay enquiries, restaurant ideas, beauty appointments, spa recommendations, family-friendly plans and local introductions.**

Less promise. More control.

## The big missing line

Add this near the top, right after the hero:

> **Hosted in Milton Keynes, The Woman Who Leads The Room is designed to bring accomplished women from across the UK into the city — supporting hotels, restaurants, retail, cultural attractions and the wider visitor economy.**

That line helps the Mayor, sponsors, hotels, restaurants and local media understand the value.
