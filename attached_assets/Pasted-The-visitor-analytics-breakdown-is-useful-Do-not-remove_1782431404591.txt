The visitor analytics breakdown is useful. Do not remove the figures, page breakdown, countries, cities, funnels or traffic source data.

Keep the detailed analytics.

What is missing is an executive layer above it.

Add a new top section called:

Executive Overview

This should sit above the charts and figures.

It should work like a senior Growth Council / Campaign Board giving the user a clear interpretation of the data.

The Executive Overview should answer:

1. What is happening?
2. What does it mean?
3. What is the risk?
4. What is the opportunity?
5. What should we do next?
6. What should we do today?
7. What should we do this week?

Use this structure:

Executive Overview

Campaign Status:
Example:
Interest is building, but conversion is weak.

What This Means:
Example:
People are visiting the I Am Her pages, but they are not yet giving contact details or taking action. The issue is not awareness. The issue is conversion.

Main Risk:
Example:
If we continue to attract visitors without capturing emails, we will lose warm interest and have no way to follow up.

Main Opportunity:
Example:
Direct traffic is high, which means people are already hearing about the brand. We should now focus on converting existing awareness into access requests, story submissions and partner enquiries.

Top 3 Actions:

1. Add a stronger lead capture offer on /iamher.
2. Improve the CTA on /access and /iamher/partnership.
3. Use UTM links on every Instagram, LinkedIn and WhatsApp post.

Today’s Priority:
Example:
Fix email capture immediately. This is the fastest way to stop losing warm visitors.

This Week’s Priority:
Example:
Run founder-led content and partnership outreach while tracking every link.

Expected Impact:
Example:
Better capture and tracking should increase measurable enquiries and show which channels are worth investing in.

Confidence Level:
Example:
High — based on 288 views and 148 sessions in the last 7 days.

Important: this should not replace the existing analytics.

The executive section should interpret the data in plain English.

The user should be able to read it in 30 seconds and understand exactly what to do.

Also fix these issues:

1. Never show NaN.
   If there is not enough data, say:
   “Not enough data yet.”

2. Replace “AI Creative Director” with:
   “Growth Advisor”
   or
   “Campaign Advisor”

3. Replace “Fix This” with:
   “Review Recommendation”
   or
   “Optimise”

4. Replace priority numbers with human-readable labels:

* Critical
* High
* Medium
* Opportunity

5. Do not recommend impossible actions.
   Example:
   If venue is TBC, do not recommend “show the venue.”

6. Recommendation cards should use this format:

Observation:
What the data says.

Meaning:
Why it matters.

Recommended Action:
What to do.

Expected Impact:
What could improve.

Confidence:
Low / Medium / High.

7. Keep all detailed figures below the Executive Overview.

The goal is:

Detailed analytics below.
Executive judgement above.

The dashboard should feel like a Growth Director is interpreting the numbers, not just displaying them.
