**PHASE 1 — Market Insight & Strategy (the wizard). Build this phase only, then stop and report.**

Phase 0 is signed off. Build Phase 1 now. Do not start any later phase. Reach the checkpoint, stop, and report back.

Reminder of the governing sequence this serves: **Research → Strategy → Audience → Sponsors → Build → Campaign → Learn.** Phase 1 is Research + Strategy. Nothing downstream (sourcing, outreach) is built yet.

**Build:**

1. A wizard-style intake page. The user enters a plain-language goal (e.g. "I am launching a women's leadership event in Milton Keynes"). The wizard asks follow-up questions when the input is incomplete — it must ask, not assume.

2. AI roles, all via OpenRouter (the key already wired in Phase 0):
   - Use **Claude** as the reasoning and conversational model — it runs the interview, asks the smart follow-up questions, and synthesises the strategy.
   - Use a **research-capable model** for the market-scan step (demand, competitors, similar events, audience pain points, relevant industries, candidate sponsor types, trending themes).
   - The wizard interviews the **user** to capture and structure what they already know. It is fast — minutes, not a research study.

3. **Juno** is the human-insight standard the wizard works to, and an optional deep-research input — it is NOT wired into the wizard and the wizard never waits on it. Leave a clean field/section in the strategy pack where Juno findings can be pasted or imported later. Do not build a Juno API connection now.

4. Output a **strategy pack** saved onto the event's config: audience personas, sponsor personas, messaging recommendations, suggested pricing, content themes, and a market-opportunity score.

5. The strategy pack is presented for **human approval** before anything uses it. The approved audience and sponsor profiles become the targeting parameters that Phase 2 will use for sourcing — store them in a structured form (job titles, sectors, locations, company size) so Phase 2 can read them directly.

**Hard rules (unchanged):** event-agnostic — the strategy attaches to an event record, nothing hardcoded. Respect the AI spend ceiling. Build only this phase.

**Checkpoint:** From a one-line goal, the wizard asks sensible clarifying questions and produces an approved strategy pack — audience and sponsor profiles, message, pricing, opportunity score — saved to the event config and structured so Phase 2 sourcing can read the targeting parameters directly. Then stop and report.

---

Two notes for you, not the Agent:

When the wizard's built, test it with the *real* I Am Her brief — your actual goal, your actual audience. That way Phase 1 doesn't just pass a technical checkpoint, it produces the genuine strategy pack you'll use to source for August. Two birds.

And watch this phase's cost the way you did Phase 0. The wizard involves more AI calls (the interview plus the market scan), so it'll likely cost more than Phase 0 did. If the burn jumps sharply, that's your signal to pause and reassess before Phase 2 — exactly the early-warning the phasing gives you.
