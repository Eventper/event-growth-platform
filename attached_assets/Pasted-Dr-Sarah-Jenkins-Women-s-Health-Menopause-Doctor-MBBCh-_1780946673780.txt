Dr Sarah Jenkins

Women's Health & Menopause Doctor

MBBCh · Fellow of the Royal Society of Medicine (FRSM) · Senior Member of the European Society of Aesthetic Gynaecology (ESAG) · Associate Member of the British College of Aesthetic Medicine (BCAM) · Affiliate Member of the Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists · UK Menopause Advocate of the Year 2025 · Safety in Beauty Rising Star Award 2024

Dr Sarah Jenkins is a multi-award-winning UK doctor and women's health advocate with over a decade of experience across NHS and private practice. Her work focuses on menopause, hormonal balance, intimate health, and the often-overlooked physical realities experienced by women in leadership. A national broadcaster — featured on This Morning, in Hello! Magazine, and on Olivia Attwood's The Price of Perfection — Dr Sarah was honoured with the Safety in Beauty Rising Star Award 2024 and UK Menopause Advocate of the Year 2025. She is a Fellow of the Royal Society of Medicine, a Senior Member of the European Society of Aesthetic Gynaecology, and an Affiliate Member of the Royal College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists. At The Woman Who Leads the Room, Dr Sarah leads the conversation on the physical and hormonal realities that shape how women lead, sustain, and stay — and what organisations need to understand about supporting them.

Esther Emenike-Okorie — Women's Health & Emotional Wellbeing
Esther Emenike-Okorie

Women's Health & Emotional Wellbeing

Registered Nurse · Health & Social Care Professional · Specialist in Women's Health & Emotional Wellbeing

Esther Emenike-Okorie is a registered nurse and health and social care professional with deep expertise in the emotional and psychological realities women face in leadership. Her work sits at the intersection of clinical care, emotional health, and the invisible demands placed on high-performing women. At The Woman Who Leads the Room, Esther brings her compassionate, evidence-based approach to the conversation on how women feel — not just how they perform. She is the wellbeing authority behind the emotional dimension of the evening: the quiet weight of responsibility, the unspoken expectations, and the restoration that comes from being truly understood.
