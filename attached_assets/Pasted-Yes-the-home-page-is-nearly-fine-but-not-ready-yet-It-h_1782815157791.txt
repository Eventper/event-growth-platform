Yes — the **home page is nearly fine**, but not ready yet.

It has the right ingredients. The problem is the **headline category is still too soft**.

Right now the page says:

> “A private luxury dinner and wellbeing evening…” 

That weakens the commercial/civic positioning. It sounds like a nice restorative evening. But the guest list you’re building is CEOs, founders, COOs, CFOs, directors and serious operators.

So the homepage should lead with **leadership dinner**, not **wellbeing evening**.

## What to change on the home page

### 1. Hero headline / subheadline

Replace the current hero line with:

**A private leadership dinner for 100 accomplished women across the UK — founders, directors, executives, business owners and senior decision-makers.**

Then underneath:

**Hosted in Milton Keynes on Friday 28 August 2026, the evening brings together women from different industries for dinner, expert conversation, editorial portraiture, visibility and strategic connection.**

This is stronger. It sells the room.

### 2. Keep the “not this” section

This bit is good:

**Not a networking event. Not a women-in-business conference. Not a room full of advice.** 

Keep it.

But add:

**Not a coaching room.**

So it becomes:

**Not a networking event.
Not a women-in-business conference.
Not a coaching room.
Not a room full of advice.**

### 3. Change “Wellbeing Session”

The programme currently calls the 20:00 section **Wellbeing Session**. 

That sounds too soft.

Change it to:

**Leadership, Health & Confidence Conversation**

Subline:

**Led by Dr Sarah Jenkins and Esther Emenike-Okorie, this conversation explores the physical, emotional and confidence realities of leading at this level.**

That keeps the value but raises the positioning.

### 4. Remove “live music” unless confirmed

The page says “Live music.” 

If it is not confirmed, change it to:

**Music. Soft light. The right mood.**

Do not overclaim.

### 5. Fix “special discounts”

The homepage says:

**Special discounts available for guests staying the weekend** 

Only keep that if confirmed.

Otherwise use:

**Guest stay options and local recommendations will be shared with confirmed guests.**

### 6. Partner section is good but too long

The partner section is strategically useful, but on the main guest page it can distract from applications. It talks about financial institutions and brand partners in detail. 

Keep it, but compress it.

Use one short section:

**Partnership Opportunities**

**We are inviting a small number of aligned financial, lifestyle, hospitality, beauty, wellness and media partners to be meaningfully present within the evening. Partnership details are available on request.**

Buttons:

**Explore Partnership**
**Email Us Directly**

That’s enough. Don’t turn the guest page into a sponsorship deck.

The homepage is **80% there**.

The gap is not design. It is category.

Right now it sounds like:

**luxury wellbeing dinner**
