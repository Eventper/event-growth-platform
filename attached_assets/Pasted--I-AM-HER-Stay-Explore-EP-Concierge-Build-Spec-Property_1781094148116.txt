**Purpose:** Turn the ticket into a weekend. Sell Milton Keynes as a destination, then capture stay-over/family/tour intent through an **EP Concierge** callback so the team can build group hotel rates on demand.
**Status:** Spec ready for build + copy ready to use. Light consent/privacy step required (§7).

---

## 0. The model (read first)

**No fixed hotel partnership.** Instead of pre-negotiating one hotel deal, we **capture demand first**, then negotiate **group rates reactively** once we know the numbers. The page inspires the weekend; the **EP Concierge button** captures the lead; a human follows up and groups bookings.

Why this way:
- No commitment or commission conversation before there's proven demand.
- EP holds the demand and approaches hotels *with numbers* — the strong negotiating position.
- It's a repeatable EP service (works for every future event), not a one-off.

**One service runs through the whole page: EP Concierge.** Every section ends with the same single call to action — *"Let our concierge handle it."*

## 0.1 Imagery & visual direction (applies to every section)

The job of the imagery here is **persuasion** — make a woman think "actually, yes, I'll make a weekend of it." Where the Story Wall is introspective, this page is the opposite: bright, alive, aspirational.

**Tone:** Vibrant, sunny, premium-travel — think destination brochure, not corporate stock. Late-August energy: blue skies, green open spaces (MK is famous for its parks and greenery), warm light. The page should feel like a place worth getting on a train for.

**It must be about the actual city.** Use real, recognisable Milton Keynes imagery — the specific attractions named on the page (Bletchley Park, Woburn Safari Park, Willen Lake and its Peace Pagoda, centre:mk, MK Gallery, Xscape), not generic "city" or "hotel room" stock. Specificity is what makes it credible and exciting. A real photo of Willen Lake at golden hour sells the weekend; an anonymous lake doesn't.

**Show the experience, not just the place.** People enjoying it — women laughing over dinner and cocktails, a family at the safari park, someone walking the lake at sunrise, friends shopping. Diverse and inclusive, matching the audience, with Black and diaspora women present throughout.

**Two moods for the two tracks:**
- *Coming for you* — elevated, stylish, restful: rooftop bar, lakeside calm, gallery, good food.
- *Bringing the family* — joyful, energetic, sunny: safari, theme park, lake activities, kids having fun.

**Sourcing & rights:** Licensed stock, commissioned shots, or — ideal — **official press/media imagery from the attractions and from Destination Milton Keynes**, which is high quality, recognisable and free to use with credit (a good reason to talk to DMK early, see §6). No scraped or unlicensed images. Credit where required.

**Performance:** All images optimised (WebP, responsive `srcset`, lazy-load below the fold), descriptive alt text for accessibility and SEO. This page is image-heavy, so performance matters — don't let it load slowly.

## 3. EP Concierge form

Triggered by the CTA button. Keep it short — the goal is the callback, not a full booking online.

| Field | Type | Rules |
|---|---|---|
| Full Name | Text | Required |
| Phone / WhatsApp number | Text | Required (this is a callback request) |
| Email | Text | Optional |
| Staying over? | Toggle / select | Yes / No / Not sure yet |
| Number of nights | Select | 1 / 2 / 3+ (show if staying over) |
| Who's coming with you? | Select | Just me / Friends or colleagues / Partner / Family with children |
| Number in your group | Number | Drives group-rate logic |
| Children & ages | Text | Optional — show if "Family with children" (tailors recommendations) |
| What can we help with? | Multi-select | Group hotel rate · Restaurant booking · Family things to do · Train tickets · Station/airport transfer · Full weekend itinerary (2–3 days) · Something else |
| Best time to call | Select | Morning / Afternoon / Evening |
| Consent | Checkbox | Required (§7) |
| Request a call | Button | Disabled until required fields + consent complete |

**Success message:**
> Thank you — your request is with the EP Concierge. We'll be in touch at your preferred time to help you plan your weekend.

## 4. Callback flow (what happens after submit)

1. Submission lands in an **EP Concierge queue** (route to the operations mailbox — adminuk@eventperfekt.com — with the Director BCC to info@eventperfekt.com per routing rules). Agent-first: auto-acknowledge instantly by email/SMS, then a human works the queue.
2. **Group-rate logic:** when several requests share dates / "staying over = yes", batch them. Once there are ~5+ beds in play, the concierge approaches the shortlisted hotels for a group rate and goes back to those guests with a code or held block. (This is the whole point of the model — group on demand, don't pre-commit.)
3. **Family requests** get the day-out recommendations matched to the children's ages.
4. **Itinerary requests** (2–3 day tour) get a simple Saturday/Sunday plan the concierge can assemble from the attraction reference (§5).
5. **Train / transfer requests** — concierge books or advises.
6. Track each request's status (New → Contacted → Booking in progress → Closed) so nothing is dropped. Keep it on the existing portal/tracker rather than a new system.

## 6. Separate note — get I Am Her listed with Destination Milton Keynes (DMK)

**For:** whoever handles partnerships / marketing.
**Why:** the analytics show a reach gap — too little real, targeted UK traffic. DMK is the city's official tourism body with engaged local social channels (#LoveMK) and an events listing. Getting the event listed there is **free or low-cost, local, and correctly targeted** — the exact audience the campaign is missing.

**Actions:**
1. Submit *I Am Her* to the **DMK events listing** (the event listing is the cheap, high-value bit — separate from any paid membership). Contact: info@destinationmiltonkeynes.co.uk.
2. Ask DMK to amplify on their social channels; tag **@DestinationMK** and use **#LoveMK** on our own posts about the event.
3. Note on membership: free DMK listings are reserved for visitor attractions, accommodation and conference venues. As an events *supplier*, EP would fall under **paid standard membership** if we want a permanent business listing — decide separately whether that's worth it. The **event listing** does not require it.
4. Use the destination page itself as a reason for DMK to engage — we're actively driving overnight visitors into the city, which is exactly DMK's stated goal (capturing more overnight tourism spend).
