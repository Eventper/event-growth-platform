I actually agree with you.

I would **not mention CEFAS by name on the public website** unless you have explicit permission. Even though it's public procurement information, using a client's name prominently in your marketing can be sensitive—particularly for government bodies.

Instead, position yourself like professional consulting firms do.

Tell Replit:

```text id="cwq5od"
Remove specific client names from the public homepage and public case studies.

Do not publicly display client names such as CEFAS unless explicit permission has been obtained.

Instead, use anonymised case studies and sector-based examples.

For example:

Public Sector Programme
UK Government Agency
Delivered a multi-country programme across nine African countries involving stakeholder engagement, in-country coordination, financial reconciliation and governance oversight.

Education Sector
International university programme delivered across multiple African countries.

Corporate Energy Programme
Cross-border stakeholder engagement and programme management.

Financial Services
Regional conference delivery and executive engagement.

Charity & Development
Multi-country workshop and capacity-building programme.

Each public case study should focus on:

- The client's challenge
- Our approach
- Countries covered
- Services delivered
- Outcomes achieved

Do not disclose:
- Client name
- Contract reference
- Contract value
- Sensitive operational information
- Financial information
- Internal governance information

Instead use phrases such as:

"UK Government Agency"

"International Development Organisation"

"Global Research Institution"

"International NGO"

"FTSE-listed Corporate"

"Multinational Energy Company"

Create a gated "Request Full Case Study" process.

Users must submit:
- Name
- Organisation
- Job Title
- Business Email
- Country
- Reason for request

Once approved, they can access more detailed case studies where appropriate.

The homepage should focus on credibility and capability rather than naming clients.

Our value proposition is our ability to deliver complex programmes across Africa—not our client list.
```

I think that's the more professional approach. It demonstrates experience without risking client relationships or creating the impression that you're using government clients as marketing assets.
